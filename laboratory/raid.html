RAID Installation

RAID (Redundant Array of Independent Disks) is a storage technology that combines multiple physical disks into logical units for improved performance, redundancy, or both. A Stripped Volume (RAID 0) splits data evenly across two or more disks, enhancing performance but offering no fault tolerance. A Mirrored Volume (RAID 1) duplicates data on two or more disks, providing high fault tolerance at the cost of reduced storage efficiency. A Spanned Volume (JBOD) combines multiple disks into a single logical volume without striping or redundancy, utilizing full disk capacity but lacking performance improvements or fault tolerance. RAID 5 stripes data and parity across three or more disks, balancing performance, storage efficiency, and fault tolerance, though it suffers from slower write speeds due to parity calculations. Each RAID level offers distinct trade-offs to suit different needs.
Instructions:
Perform the RAID installation of the following level:
- Stripped Volume
- Mirrored Volume
- Spanned Volume
- RAID 5
